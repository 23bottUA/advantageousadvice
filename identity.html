Your Gifts

Identity, December 2, 2022

"Since we have gifts that differ according to the grace given to us, let us exercise them: if prophecy, in proportion to the faith; if ministry, in ministering; if one is a teacher, in teaching; if one exhorts, in exhortation; if one contributes, in generosity; if one is over others, with diligence; if one does acts of mercy, with cheerfulness." Romans 12:6-8

High school years were full of self-discovery and finding our place in the world. We joined clubs that matched our interests, we made friends (maybe changed friends over the years), and so much more. Sometimes, we place our identities in objects outside ourselves. The best way to discover your identity is through examining your own mind.

To better understand your personality traits and God-given gifts, we should all take the time to a) read Scripture and b) find our Enneagram types. There are 9 Enneagram types, some overlapping and others completely different. Each consists of a list of traits and gifts, strengths and weaknesses of each personality. The video below describes the Enneagram well.

The Enneagram Explained

You can take the quiz here: https://www.truity.com/test/enneagram-personality-test.

Finding your Enneagram type will help you fully uncover and reflect on your gifts to be able to use them well in your classes, service, and relationships. When you know yourself, you feel more comfortable in your own skin and can more freely express yourself. Not fully knowing yourself leads to hardships in college life and beyond. If you don’t know who you are, you struggle with being confident, building solid relationships, and revealing your personality to the world. The Enneagram is not the solution to this, but it may help you in your journey through it.

I wish you good luck in your search for yourself and good grades in your classes!
