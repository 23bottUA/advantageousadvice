The Dating Scene

Dating, December 11, 2022

"And above all these put on love, which binds everything together in perfect harmony." Colossians 3:14

During your college years, you may have many a person approach you and ask you out. Depending on what you want in a relationship, you may decide to say yes. However, there are certain types of people you should look out for. You don’t want to end up a victim of manipulation, so these are a few people who you should be wary of.

  • The Flip-Flopper: the off-and-on partner
  • The Problem Child/the Fixer-Upper: the “I can fix them.” Honey, no, you can’t.
  • The Walking Hormone: loves self-gratification at expense of their partner; manipulates through pressure or guilt
  • The Control Freak: controls who you spend time with, where you go, what you do, etc.
  • The Potty Mouth: insulting; verbally abusive
  • The Tearful Cheater: comes running back apologizing for cheating when they’re not truly sorry

A great way to avoid these kinds of partners is to set high standards and stick to them. Never settle. I once settled, and that relationship barely lasted a few months. Despite what Nike tells you, just don’t do it.

Make a list of characteristics you want in a significant other. Add onto this list as you date people and realize what kind of person you would want to be with forever.

One more thing to do when dating someone, or when in a friendship, is set boundaries. If they do not respect your boundaries, they are not worth your time. Yet again, I learned this the hard way. Do not repeat my mistakes and set clear boundaries. Never change your boundaries for anyone if those boundaries are important to you.

Make sure the people you spend time with respect you and fully care for you. Pure love has its challenges, but it is totally rewarding.
