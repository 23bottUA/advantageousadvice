Building Good Friendships

Friendships, December 5, 2022

"Some friends bring ruin on us, but a true friend is more loyal than a brother." Proverbs 18:24

They say high school and college are times to find your bridesmaids, not your groom. But that’s a lot easier said than done. I switched friend groups three times by senior year at Ursuline. They were definitely some of the hardest things I went through during high school, but I came to realize that some friendships weren’t meant to be.

When I learned the three types of friendships, they helped me realize why those friendships didn’t necessarily work out. Those types are 1) friendships of utility, 2) friendships of pleasure, and 3) virtuous friendships.

Friendships of utility are like the relationship you have with the barista at the coffee shop. They make you coffee, you pay for it. It’s a simple give-and-take relationship. There’s no deep conversations or connections in this friendship.

Friendships of pleasure are with those people you like to hang out with, maybe go to a party with. You’ll chat it up with them, but you don’t feel fulfilled or challenged in this friendship. You hang out with them because they please you.

Virtuous friendships are those deep, meaningful relationships with people. They could be with your best friend, the person you may date, or family members. They challenge you to grow and stay with you through thick and thin.

Tina Augustine gives a great talk about friendships to help you better understand the different types.

After learning the types of friendships, I realized that many of mine had been of utility or pleasure. I had grown up thinking that people would only be friends with me if I could do something for them because that’s just how I was treated. I felt like a calculator at times, taken out and used when someone needed the answer to something and put back into the bag for later. I knew I wanted real virtuous friendships in my life and realized I had some right in front of me.

Sometimes deciding who your real friends are takes some deep reflection. It’s good to analyze some of your relationships to decide if they’re of utility, pleasure, or virtue. My own reflections helped me feel more confident and secure in my friendships, more than I had ever been.
